What materials are dishwasher safe and why to avoid others?

Making sure the materials in your kitchen can handle a good wash in the dishwasher can help make sure they stay in top shape for years.

Here's a list of the materials that are safe for dishwasher use and why you should avoid others:

  • Melamine: Melamine is a very durable material that can handle quite a bit of heat and a good wash without deteriorating.
  • Stainless steel: Thanks to its durable and heat-resistant nature, stainless steel is a great option for items that need to be dishwasher-safe.
  • Plastic: Some plastic items, such as plates and cups, are relatively safe to put in the dishwasher. However, it's important to be careful when selecting plastic items, as some may melt in the dishwasher.
  • Cast iron: Cast iron cookware should not be put in the dishwasher, as it can rust and corrode with repeated exposure to the extreme heat and soap.
  • Wooden or food-grade silicone utensils: Wood and food-grade silicone utensils should not be put in the dishwasher, as they can warp and become brittle from the heat and pressure.
  • Painted glassware: Painted glassware should not be put in the dishwasher, as the paint may wear away due to the strong detergents used.

How to properly load Kong Toy in a dishwasher?

Keeping your Kong Toy squeaky clean is an important part of keeping your pup healthy and happy.

Fortunately, cleaning your Kong Toy with a dishwasher is a quick and easy way to do just that! Here's how you do it:

  • Remove any larger pieces of food stuck to the inside of the Kong.
  • Pre-rinse the Kong off with lukewarm water to remove any loose dirt and debris.
  • Place the Kong Toy in the top rack of your dishwasher, making sure it's not stacked on top of any other dishes.
  • Add some dishwasher soap and run the machine on the normal cycle.
  • Once washed, inspect the Kong Toy for any dirt or debris. If needed, you may need to scrub it again with a soft brush.
  • Let the Kong Toy cool down before giving it back to your pup.

Following these steps will help you properly and effectively clean your Kong Toy with a dishwasher.

Alternatives to dishwasher cleaning for Kong Toy:

  • Boiling: Boiling your Kong toys in a pot of boiling water is an easy and effective way to remove stubborn dirt and food particles. Safety Tip: Be sure to have pet-safe liquid detergents and/or salt handy to prevent the water from boiling over.
  • Soaking: Soaking your Kong Toy in an enzyme-based solution or dish soap and hot water can help to remove tough grime without damaging the toy. Soak your Kong Toy for about 15 minutes for best results.
  • Rubbing Alcohol: Blending rubbing alcohol, water, and dish soap together can create a powerful and highly effective cleaning solution. Safety Tip: Be sure to cover up any openings in the Kong Toy before using rubbing alcohol to avoid damage from liquids entering the toy.
  • Vinegar and Baking Soda: Mixing vinegar and baking soda together can create a mild, safe solution for cleaning your Kong Toy. Scrub the Kong Toy in the mixture, then rinse with water afterwards to remove any residue.

These alternative methods of cleaning your Kong Toy are just as effective as conventional dishwasher cleaning, depending on the level of dirt and grime on the toy.

Whichever method you choose, be sure to let your Kong Toy air dry after cleaning it to ensure that the material stays in good condition. Happy cleaning!

What are the Safety issues with cleaning Kong Toys in a dishwasher?

Cleaning your pet's Kong Toy in a dishwasher can be an easy way to keep the toy sanitized, however it's important to first consider the safety implications before you set out to do so.

Here are some of the safety issues you may want to keep in mind while cleaning Kong Toy in a dishwasher:

  • The rubber material of Kong Toy can expand when exposed to heat. If your dishwasher is too hot, the toy may be damaged beyond repair.
  • Harsh soaps, detergents, and sanitizers can be present in a dishwasher, which could be abrasive against Kong Toy, resulting in potential damage to the toy.
  • In a warm and moist environment, a dishwasher can promote bacterial and mould growth. This is why it's important to air-dry the toy after washing.
  • The dishwasher may not reach a high enough temperature to truly sanitize the toy. As such, other methods of cleaning may be more effective for each individual pet owner.

Overall, it's important to consider potential safety issues before cleaning Kong Toy in a dishwasher.
