What materials are dishwasher safe and why to avoid others?

When it comes to dishwashers, certain types of materials hold up better to the washing process than others.

Here is a list of materials that are considered to be dishwasher-safe and why it is important to avoid certain materials, such as those that are not heat or water resistant:

  • Glass: Glass is an excellent material to use in a dishwasher as it is extremely heat, water and chemical resistant, making it a great material for withstanding high temperatures and harsh cleaning agents.
  • Aluminum: Aluminum is also a great material for the dishwasher, as it holds up well to heat and water and will not corrode or corrode quickly when exposed to harsh cleaning agents.
  • Stainless Steel: Stainless Steel is a great choice for the dishwasher, as it is extremely heat resistant and will not corrode or rust when washed in the dishwasher.
  • Plastic: Plastic is another good option for the dishwasher, as it is heat and water resistant. However, some plastics may not be able to withstand the harsh cleaning agents used in the dishwasher and may need to be washed by hand.

It is important to avoid materials that are not heat or water resistant in a dishwasher, such as wood or paper.

These materials can be very easily damaged by the heat and water of the dishwasher and may end up causing more harm than good.

How to properly load Avent Bottles in a dishwasher?

Do you have Avent Bottles that need washing, but you're not sure how to do it without damaging them?

Don't worry – with a few simple steps and tips, you can easily and safely load Avent Bottles in a dishwasher.

Here's what you need to know to get the job done right:

  • Choose the right dishwasher setting: If your dishwasher has a “gentle” or “eco” cycle, use it to prevent damage to the bottles.
  • Turn the caps and nipples: Make sure the cap and nipple are facing down to avoid water pooling.
  • Load them separately: Load them in separate compartments or baskets to prevent pieces getting tangled during the washing cycle.
  • Keep them up and away from the heating element: Place them on the top rack, as close as possible to the middle, so that the bottles don’t touch the heating element.
  • Avoid detergents with bleach: Use a mild detergent and avoid one that contains bleach.

Following these tips will ensure that your Avent Bottles are properly cleaned in the dishwasher without any damage.

Enjoy your sparkling clean bottles!

What are the Safety issues with cleaning Avent Bottles in a dishwasher?

It's completely normal to want to make cleaning and sterilizing your baby's Avent bottles easier.

Cleaning and sterilizing them in the dishwasher is a great option as it saves time - but it's important to take certain steps to ensure your little one's safety.

Here's a quick guide to the safety issues you should keep in mind when cleaning your Avent Bottles in a dishwasher:

  • Use a Drying Rack: Place your Avent Bottles onto a separate drying rack in the top basket of the dishwasher instead of directly onto the shelves. This way you can ensure that your bottles don't come in contact with any detergent.
  • Avoid High Temperatures: Set your dishwasher to a low-heat cycle to avoid exposing your Avent bottles to high temperatures which can affect their structure over time.
  • Clean Out the Soap Dispenser: Make sure to clean out the soap dispenser of your dishwasher before putting your Avent bottles in. This can help to ensure that no residual detergent is left in the machine which could harm your baby.
  • Skip the Rinse Aid: Avoid using rinse aids such as vinegar or JetDry as they can cause staining on your Avent baby bottles.

By taking these steps, you can keep your little one safe while also making cleaning and sterilizing your Avent bottles easier.
