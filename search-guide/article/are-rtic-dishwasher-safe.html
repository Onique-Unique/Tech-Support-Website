What materials are dishwasher safe and why to avoid others?

Dishwashers are an essential part of our daily lives as they are useful in quickly, easily, and efficiently cleaning our dishes and utensils.

Knowing which materials are safe to put in the dishwasher is key to avoiding damage and optimizing the cleaning process.

To help determine which materials are safe and which to avoid, here are the characteristics that make a material dishwasher safe and why to avoid certain materials:

  • Materials such as metal, glass, plastic, and ceramic are generally considered dishwasher safe because they have strong durability and heat resistance.
  • Metal is particularly resistant to corrosion, making it safe to be cleaned without harsh chemicals.
  • Glass is also dishwasher safe and its heat resistance makes it suitable for a wash with hot water.
  • Plastic is also typically dishwasher safe as most of them can withstand high temperatures and heavy pressure.
  • Ceramic is also a popular choice for dishwasher safe as it has strong durability and is a tough material.
  • Materials such as wood and rubber should be avoided in the dishwasher because they are not heat resistant and can be ruined by the combination of high temperature and moisture.
  • They can also be damaged by harsh detergents or the powerful jets of a dishwasher. Similarly, delicate materials such as fine china, crystal, and silver should be avoided in the dishwasher as they could be damaged by intense heat and moisture as well.

In short, most metals, glass, plastic, and ceramic are typically safe to be put in a dishwasher.

However, wood, rubber, delicate materials such as fine china, crystal, and silver should be avoided as they could be easily damaged by the intense heat and moisture of a dishwasher.
