Where to buy non slip Nikes?

How to know if Nikes are non slip?

Here are what to look for to know if Nikes are non slip:

  • Look at the bottom of the shoe for a unique patterned tread. Non-slip Nikes are typically designed with special grooves and raised circles that help you grip the surface you're walking on.
  • Check the material composition of the shoe. Non-slip Nikes are typically composed of rubberized materials for maximum traction.
  • Measure the thickness of the sole. Nike's non-slip shoes have a thicker sole than their other models to ensure extra grip when you're out and about.
  • Take a look at the heel. Depending on the type of non-slip Nike you're looking at, the heel may have additional traction to enhance your grip.
  • Read the description of the shoe. Nike often provides details about the type of non-slip technology used in their shoes. This can help you determine whether or not they are truly non-slip.



Non slip shoe materials and features:

Non-slip shoes are an important choice for anyone who needs to remain stable on their feet in slippery or wet conditions like those found in slippery kitchens, medical facilities, and healthcare centers.

Depending on the surface, different materials and features can be used to create shoes that offer a secure grip, improved traction, and stability.

To select the right non-slip shoe material and features, it is important to consider the context in which they will be worn.

One of the most commonly used materials to make non-slip shoes is leather. Leather is naturally oil and water-resistant, and it can be treated with special slip-resistant surfaces to make it even more so.

The treated surface creates a rough texture that offers more traction on slippery surfaces. Synthetic materials like polyurethane are also used in the manufacturing of non-slip shoes. Polyurethane is extremely durable, and it is often seen on shoes for medical professionals.

When selecting non-slip shoes, it is important to also consider features like a non-skid sole, an ergonomically designed footbed, and a cushioned midsole. Non-skid soles are designed to provide more grip on wet surfaces, perfect for medical professionals who are regularly exposed to water and other fluids.


An ergonomically designed footbed is important because it keeps the foot from slipping within the shoe and keeps it in a secure position so that the shoe remains stable at all times. Lastly, a cushioned midsole prevents fatigue and further increases the comfort of wearing the shoe.

Choosing the right non-slip shoe material and features is key to staying safe in wet or slippery environments. With the right knowledge and selection, you can easily find the best kind of non-slip shoe to suit your unique needs.

How to care for and maintain non slip shoes?

Here are some valuable tips to consider on how to take care of your Nike non slip shoes:

  • Rotate your non-slip shoes regularly. Have multiple pairs and alternate between them so that any one pair is not worn for too long and will not get worn out causing the tread to diminish.
  • Clean and maintain your non-slip shoes regularly to ensure that no dirt or debris accumulate on the sole and the shoe material does not become damaged or discolored due to moisture or direct sunlight.
  • Use a waterproofing spray to keep your non-slip shoes from getting damaged by rain and snow.
  • If you notice any signs of wear on your non-slip shoes, such as scuff marks or worn tread, take them to a shoe repair shop to make sure they are still safe and effective.
  • Store your non-slip shoes in a cool, dry area and away from direct sunlight to prevent discoloration and premature wear.
  • Avoid walking on slick surfaces in your non-slip shoes. While the tread is designed to provide some slip-resistant qualities, walking on wet, icy or otherwise slick surfaces can cause your tread to wear out more quickly.
  • Replace your non-slip shoes once they start to lose their grip and slip resistance as this is an indication of worn tread.
