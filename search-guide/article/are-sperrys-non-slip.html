Non slip shoe materials and features:

Non slip shoes are designed to provide improved traction and stability on slippery surfaces.

They are a popular choice for anyone who works in frequently wet or icy environments, or who are prone to slips and falls.

Non slip shoes can be made from a variety of materials, each offering its own unique set of benefits.

Rubber is one of the most commonly used materials to make non slip shoes, due to its natural grip and durability. Many non slip shoes are made with PVC or polyurethane soles, which provide a stronger non slip grip than rubber, while remaining flexible and lightweight.

Other shoe materials for non slip shoes include neoprene, canvas and leather. Neoprene is a synthetic rubber-like material that is lightweight, warm and waterproof. Canvas shoes are often coated with a water-resistant material and their soft texture allows for superb grip in wet conditions. Leather is also a popular choice for non slip shoes, as its dense and durable structure provides the foot with maximum support and stability.

Non slip shoe features including reinforced toe areas, improved cushioning and anti-microbial linings provide further protection and comfort in wet and slippery conditions.


Some non slip shoes feature protective rubber or plastic toe guards, which help protect the tops of the feet from moisture and debris. With the right non slip shoes, you can stay safe and confident even on the most slippery surfaces.

What are the Safety issues with Sperry Shoes?

Sperry shoes have been a staple of any spring/summer wardrobe for decades, but it's important to consider their potential safety issues before wearing them.

  • One major safety issue with wearing Sperry shoes is their relatively poor traction on wet and slippery surfaces. The sole of a Sperry features rubber studs called siping, which are designed to increase traction whilst ensuring they remain lightweight and flexible.
  • However, in rainy or wet conditions these rubber studs can become inefficient in providing secure grip. This can make walking down an inclined surface hazardous and lead to slips and falls.
  • Another safety concern with Sperry shoes is the lacing system. It is important to ensure that the laces are firmly secured and aren't left loose, as this can cause tripping. Also, given the fragile nature of the leather upper, care should be taken when pressure is applied to the material, such as when engaging in any vigorous activity. It is also important not to subject sperrys to any abrasive surfaces, as this may damage the material and lead to premature wear.
  • On the plus side, Sperry shoes are usually lightweight and feature good arch support, which can make them a comfortable option, especially for extended periods of walking. They are also generally resistant to water and other liquids.

In conclusion, it is important to be aware of the potential safety issues before wearing sperry shoes, such as lack of traction in wet conditions and the potential for lacing failure.

However, when worn with the necessary precautions, Sperrys can still be a comfortable and stylish addition to any summer wardrobe.

Are Sperry Topsiders non slip?

Are All Sperry's non slip?

No, not all Sperry's are non-slip.

While many of the popular boat shoes that Sperry produces and sells are designed with a non-skid rubber sole, this isn't the case for all of the brand's shoes.

Sperry also produces sandals and other flats, many of which neither feature the classic slip-resistant sole, nor are they water-resistant.
