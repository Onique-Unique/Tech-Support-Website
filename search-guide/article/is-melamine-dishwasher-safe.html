Is Melamine Dinnerware dishwasher safe?

Yes, melamine dinnerware is dishwasher safe under certain conditions.

Generally speaking, as long as the items are labeled "dishwasher safe" they can be safely washed in the dishwasher.

However, melamine dinnerware can typically only handle a low-to-medium heat setting and established care instructions should be followed to ensure dishes don't become damaged or warped.

Additionally, melamine dinnerware should not be placed in a preheated dishwasher.


It's also important to avoid mixing melamine dishes with other types of dinnerware, as the high temperatures and abrasive detergents can corrode the items and cause them to become dull in color.

How to properly load Melamine in a dishwasher?

With the correct steps, it is possible to safely and effectively clean your Melamine plates, bowls and other items in a dishwasher.

Here are tips and instructions on how to properly load Melamine in a dishwasher:

  • Presoak Melamine items in hot, soapy water before placing them in the dishwasher. This loosens any stuck on bits of food and will help achieve a better clean.
  • Place Melamine items on the top rack, making sure that each item is not touching or overlapping. This allows the spray arms to reach all parts and eliminates the risk of Melamine items sticking together from the heat of the dishwasher.
  • Use the delicate cycle setting and set the water temperature to low to avoid cracking or warping the Melamine.
  • Use mild detergent to help get Melamine clean, such as a dish washer detergent specifically designed for washing Melamine.
  • Once the cycle has been completed, dry Melamine items with a soft cloth.

Following these steps will ensure that your Melamine items come out of the dishwasher clean, undamaged and good as new.

How to care and maintain Melamine after dishwasher cleaning?

Everyone loves the convenience of pre-washed Melamine dishes and bowls, but to ensure they last, it's important to care for them properly.

Here are a few tips on how to maintain Melamine after it has been cleaned in a dishwasher and prevent damage, discoloration, fading, or warping that may happen over time:

  • Dry Melamine dishes and bowls completely with a soft cloth after each cycle to avoid water damage or stains.
  • Immediately store the dishes and bowls in a cool, dry area.
  • Avoid using abrasive cleaning products or clashing Melamine dishes together when hand-washing.
  • Do not stack Melamine dishes on top of each other; instead, use a wire rack or plate stand to help them air out.
  • Check for chips or cracks in the dishes, and repair any problems right away.
  • Avoid extreme temperatures; reheating food in the microwave using Melamine dishes could cause warping.
  • Whenever possible, avoid using the dishwasher after the first several washings.
  • Periodically inspect for signs of wear and tear, and replace worn or chipped dishes.

What are the Safety issues with cleaning Melamine in a dishwasher?

Melamine dishes provide an elegant and economical alternative to traditional tableware, however, there are certain safety issues you should consider before placing these dishes in a dishwasher.

With this in mind, here is a list of safety issues you should be aware of when cleaning Melamine in a dishwasher:

  • Melamine is a porous material, making it susceptible to warping, cracking and bursting over time if exposed to excessive heat or high water pressure in a dishwasher.
  • The chemicals and detergents used in dishwashers can dull the finish of Melamine dishes, leaving them looking faded and worn.
  • If Melamine dishes contain painted designs, the detailing can run or become blurred if washed in a dishwasher.
  • It is also important to make sure Melamine dishes are properly stacked in the dishwasher to avoid them clinking together, which can cause chips and breaks.

By taking these simple precautions, you can avoid any unnecessary damage and ensure that your Melamine remains in good condition for a long time to come.
