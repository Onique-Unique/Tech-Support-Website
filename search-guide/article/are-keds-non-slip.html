How to know if Keds are non slip?

Keds sneakers have been the classic slip-on sneaker to hit the scene since 1916, with a variety of styles and colors.

As Keds are primarily a fashion shoe, they have become popularized in the food service and health care industries, as they are a stylish shoe that also serves an important purpose of providing slip resistance.

If you’re looking for non-slip Keds, there are several features to look for in a shoe to ensure the right level of foot protection:

  • High Traction Outsole: The outsole or bottom of the shoe is the most important part of the shoe if slip-resistance is a deciding factor. Look for a design in the sole that has increased grip and traction.
  • Abrasion Protection: The rubber material that makes up the sole should also be durable and resistant to wear and tear. This helps to ensure the traction isn’t lost over time and that the sole performs as expected.
  • Durable Materials: The upper top part of the shoe should also be created from materials that are hard wearing and endure heavy use. By selecting Keds constructed from quality materials, you can get a well-crafted shoe that lasts.
  • Reinforced Heel: Slip-on Keds should also have a reinforced heel to provide extra cushioning and underfoot support. This can help to reduce the risk of slips, trips and falls by providing improved stability when walking or standing.
  • Moisture Management: If working in wet conditions, Keds with moisture management features is essential to ensure the foot remains dry and protected from wet floors. Look for moisture wicking lining to keep your feet comfortable during long shifts.

By ensuring your Keds meet all the above criteria, you can get the right level of slip-resistance for your working environment.

Non-slip Keds can provide the level of protection and comfort needed for long shifts and demanding job roles.

How to care for and maintain non slip shoes?

Every job comes with different footwear needs, and for some professions, non slip shoes are a must.

It’s important to maintain these shoes for both safety reasons and for a long-lasting product.

To help ensure your non slip shoes are in great shape, here are some maintenance and care tips:

  • Clean and Dry: Keeping your non slip shoes clean is a must. Be sure to clean them regularly to remove dirt and other materials that can wear down the material. Make sure to always dry them properly after cleaning or after being exposed to wet conditions.
  • Replace Soles: When the soles of your non slip shoes begin to show signs of wear, it’s important to replace them. This can help ensure the shoes are still providing good traction and a solid grip.
  • Check Treads: In between cleanings and soles replacements, be sure to look over the treads of your shoes. If you notice any build-up or tears, it’s time get it checked out and potentially replace the shoes.
  • Storing Your Shoes: When it’s time to store your non slip shoes, make sure they're not being stored in a humid area. This can help ensure the materials last longer.

By following these tips, you can ensure your non slip shoes continue to provide safety and a long lifespan.

What are the Safety issues with Keds?

Keds are a popular choice of lightweight and comfortable casual shoes.

However, just like any type of footwear, they can present their own set of safety risks.

Keds are made of lightweight fabric and can easily slip off the foot. This can be very hazardous when climbing a flight of stairs or running in them, as they can easily trip one up and lead to a terrible fall.

Moreover, the sole of Keds is relatively thin and does not provide much shock absorption or cushioning as compared to sturdier, rubber-soled shoes.

This can lead to long-term damage to the feet, whether it is due to discomfort or absorbing shock from impacting the ground too hard.

Lastly, Keds lack any waterproofing technology and can easily become waterlogged if one steps in a puddle, resulting in a loss of grip and stability with every walking step.


Staying safe when it comes to wearing Keds means exercising caution when wearing them for activities like running, climbing stairs, and steering clear of wet areas.
