What materials are dishwasher safe and why to avoid others?

Dishwashers are an integral part of any modern kitchen. But not all materials are dishwasher safe.

In order to keep your dishwasher operating properly and your dishes safe, you must be aware of which materials you can and can't put in.

Here are the qualities of a few materials that make them dishwasher safe, as well as a few to avoid:

  • Melamine: Durable and heat-resistant, melamine is one of the most commonly used materials for dinnerware, making it a dishwasher safe option.
  • Porcelain: Porcelain dishes are heat-resistant and strong, meaning they're suitable for the dishwasher.
  • Stainless Steel: Stainless steel is heat-resistant, so it won't warp or discolor in the dishwasher.
  • Wood: Wood dinnerware is not dishwasher safe because the heat and moisture can warp and crack it.
  • Hand-painted Pottery: Hand-painted pottery should not be put in the dishwasher, as the dishwasher's heat can cause the paint to fade or wear off.
  • Crystal: Crystal is not a dishwasher-safe material as the heat and moisture can cause it to warp, chip, or crack.

What are the Safety issues with cleaning MAM Bottles in a dishwasher?

Cleaning MAM Bottles in a dishwasher can exposing you and your baby to potential safety risks.

Whether you're a new parent getting ready for your little one's arrival, or a seasoned mommy just refreshing your knowledge, here are some safety issues to keep in mind when cleaning MAM Bottles in a dishwasher:

  • Heat damage: Plastics can be damaged by heat. High water temperature and the direct heat from the washing cycle can potentially damage your MAM bottles.
  • Too much detergent or bleach: Too much detergent or bleach can leave residues on the bottle, which can be ingested by your baby.
  • Damage to shaped teats: If the shaped teats of your MAM bottles are exposed to the cleaning cycle, they may be distorted or damaged.
  • Damage to valves: It is possible for the valves in the bottles to become damaged or dislodged when exposed to the cleaning cycle of the dishwasher.

It is important to remember that safety should always come first when it comes to your baby.

If you choose to use a dishwasher to clean MAM bottles, be sure to read the instructions and follow them carefully.
