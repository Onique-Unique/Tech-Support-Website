What materials are dishwasher safe and why to avoid others?

Dishwasher safe materials often possess certain common characteristics that make them resistant to heat and other elements.

Heat resistance and durability are the two key traits that make a material dishwasher safe.

Here are some of the materials you can safely put in the dishwasher and others you should avoid:

  • Plastic: Plastic is an excellent choice of material for the dishwasher. It's resistant to heat, scratches, and stains and won't warp or discolor.
  • Stainless Steel: Stainless steel is renowned for its superior heat and rust resistance, making it an ideal candidate for being washed in the dishwasher.
  • Glass and Ceramic: Glass and ceramic materials are both dishwasher safe, but require careful hand washing for longer life.
  • Silicone: Silicone's malleability and non-porous surface make it perfect for the dishwasher.
  • Wooden: Wooden utensils and kitchenware can be damaged easily by the heat and water in the dishwasher.
  • Copper: Copper is prone to rusting and discoloring when washed in the dishwasher, so it's best to stick to hand washing for such items.
  • Aluminum: Aluminum can corrode due to the high temperatures and harsh detergents of the dishwasher, and should be washed by hand.

Overall, dishwasher safe materials require specific traits to be washed and rinsed using a dishwasher.

Plastic, stainless steel, ceramic, and silicone are some of the best materials for being put in the dishwasher.

In contrast, wooden, aluminum, and copper should be avoided in the dishwasher as they are vulnerable to damage or discoloration.

What makes a product dishwasher safe?

When purchasing a product, it’s important to consider whether it is dishwasher safe or not.

Even though products are labeled as “dishwasher safe” it doesn’t automatically mean that it’s safe for all dishwashers.

Here are some characteristics to consider when determining whether a product can be safely washed in a dishwasher:

  • Resistance to Heat: Heat is one of the primary elements in a dishwasher as it is what helps kill bacteria, breaks down grease and food particles and helps clean dishes. If a product can’t withstand the temperatures inside a dishwasher, it will be damaged.
  • Resistance to Detergents: Many detergents contain chemicals that can damage certain materials. This is especially true for wooden utensils, cloth products and products with painted surfaces. If a product is not resistant to detergents, it could become damaged during the washing cycle.
  • Ability to Drain Water: Dishwashers drain water at a very high pressure, which can be too powerful for some materials. If a product cannot withstand this pressure, it could cause damage to the product or the dishwasher.
  • Material of the Product: Certain materials are not suitable for dishwashers as they can cause scratches to the surfaces of dishes or become damaged in the process. For example, wooden or plastic utensils can easily become damaged in a dishwasher and should be washed by hand.

By considering these characteristics and factors, you can determine if a product is able to be safely washed in the dishwasher.

What are the Safety issues with cleaning GFUEL Shaker in a dishwasher?

When considering how to clean a GFUEL Shaker, it's important to understand the safety issues associated with cleaning it in a dishwasher.

Below is a list of safety issues to be aware of before opting to use your dishwasher to clean your shaker:

  • The hot water and steam from the dishwasher can cause the plastic lid to warp and leak.
  • The intense pressure of the dishwasher can loosen the tamper-proof lid and the impact of the dishes against the shaker can cause cracks in the plastic.
  • The ingestion of detergent and soap residue left in the shaker can cause stomach and gastrointestinal upset from prolonged/repeated ingestion.
  • The combination of high heat, soap and pressure can cause deformation of the shape and appearance of the product.
  • Dishwasher detergents may discolor or corrode the inner components of the GFUEL Shaker and with daily use, it can reduce the life of the product.

It is always best to read the instructions of the product and follow the instructions for the best and safest results.

In this case, hand washing is the safest, most effective way to clean a GFUEL Shaker.
