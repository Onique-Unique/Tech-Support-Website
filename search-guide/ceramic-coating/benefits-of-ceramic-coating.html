Ceramic Coating: The Benefits of Protective Paint!

By applying a ceramic coating it essentially acts as a protective layer of paint that is applied to a surface. This sort of coating is commonly used on automobiles because it protects the paint from weathering and fading.

A ceramic coating has massive benefits that protect your car's paint exterior, including protection from the environment, increased durability, and a longer lifespan for the painted surface.



What is ceramic coating?

Ceramic coating is an innovative technique designed to maintain and improve the appearance of vehicle paintwork. It is a spray-on, polymer-based coating that forms a robust, permanent layer on the surface of the paint, protecting it from scratches, swirl marks, water spots, rock chips, and other types of damage.

Ceramic coating also improves the gloss and depth of color of the paint, as well as making the paint surface resistant to water and grime.

How is ceramic coating applied?

There are a few ways that ceramic coating can be applied, but the most common is by spraying it on. The coating is sprayed on in a thin, even layer and then baked on at high temperatures. This creates a hard, durable finish that can protect your car from rust and corrosion.

It can be applied as a clear coat or mixed with other color elements to match surface paint. It is best advised to remove any particles, chemical, debris etc by washing before attempting to apply ceramic coating.

A wax or waxing procedure can then be implemented to overlay the ceramic coating once it is cured so the surface can look good and new for years saving time and money.



What are the costs associated with ceramic coating?

Ceramic coatings have a wide range of prices, depending on the type of coating material and the size of the surface area to be coated. The cost of a ceramic coating can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars.

One of the major factors that affects the price of a ceramic coating is the type of material being used. More expensive materials, such as diamond-like carbon (DLC) coatings, will typically cost more than less expensive materials, such as silicon-based coatings.

The size of the surface area to be coated is another major factor that affects the cost of a ceramic coating. Larger surface areas will generally cost more to coat than smaller surface areas.

Other factors that can affect the cost of a ceramic coating include the application method, the type of substrate being coated, and the level of protection desired.

Overall, the cost of a ceramic coating will vary depending on the material used, the size of the surface area, and other factors which can range anywhere from $200 to over $1000. However, ceramic coatings are typically more affordable than other coating materials, such as paint or metal plating.
