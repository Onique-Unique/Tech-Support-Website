Common myths about Intermittent Fasting and the science behind it:

  • Myth: Intermittent fasting is just another fad diet.
  • Truth: Intermittent fasting (IF) is a scientifically backed approach to health and wellness that has been around for centuries. There is a growing body of evidence that suggests IF provides numerous health benefits. Studies suggest that IF can help you lose weight and improve cognitive function, reduce cancer risk, and reduce markers of chronic disease.
  • Myth: Youâll go into starvation mode if you practice intermittent fasting.
  • Truth: Starvation mode is a myth and is not in any way supported by science. Intermittent fasting is a natural cycle of eating and abstaining from food. The body can easily adapt to short periods of fasting and use the periods in between meals to secrete hormones, such as glucagon, which actually help with fat burning.
  • Myth: Intermittent fasting is only for weight loss.
  • Truth: Intermittent fasting is about much more than just weight loss. Studies have shown that in addition to weight loss, IF can improve many health markers, such as reducing fasting blood sugar, promoting cell repair, and reducing inflammation. It can also reduce your risk of developing metabolic diseases such as type 2 diabetes.



Different methods of Intermittent Fasting and which one may be best for you:

If you're looking for a convenient and flexible way to cleanse your body and lose some weight, intermittent fasting might be a great option for you.

Intermittent fasting is a dietary pattern where one cycles between periods of eating and fasting. Generally, it involves fasting for 16 hours out of the day and then eating all meals within the remaining 8 hours.

There are several different methods of intermittent fasting, and each has its own unique approach. Hereâs a breakdown of the different types of fasting you can try and which may be best for you:

  • 16/8 Fasting: This is the most popular method of intermittent fasting. This involves fasting for 16 hours in a day and consuming all meals within the remaining 8 hours. Eating only within a certain window such as 12noon to 8pm) works best.
  • 5:2 Fasting: This type of fasting requires you to consume 500-600 calories on two non-consecutive days and then eat normally on the other five days of the week.
  • One Meal A Day (OMAD): As the name implies, you would only consume one meal a day with this fasting plan. This meal should be nutrient-dense and provide all the essential vitamins and minerals required for the day.
  • Alternate-Day Fasting: This involves alternating between days of complete fasting and days of normal eating habit.
  • Time-Restricted Eating: This involves limiting eating to certain hours of the day, such as between 12 noon and 8 pm. This method is great for those looking to simplify their diets and lose weight.

Now you know the difference between the various types of intermittent fasting, you can decide which one would work best for you.

The key is to find one that fits your lifestyle and schedule.

So, take your time and identify the best approach that suits you and make it part of your daily routine.
