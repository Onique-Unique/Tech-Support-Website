Handling Flight Stress!

Flying can be a stressful experience, but there are several things you can do to help you feel more relaxed and prepared.

Consider researching the airport you will be flying into, bring comfortable clothing to wear on the flight, and bring snacks or entertainment to help pass the time. It's also important to allow plenty of time to get to the airport and to check in, as rushing can increase stress levels.

When you're preparing for a long flight, there are certain essential items you should always bring to ensure maximum comfort and make your trip more enjoyable.

Here are some of our top picks for your flight checklist:

  • Comfortable clothes: Choose clothing that's breathable and comfortable, and don't forget an extra layer in case the plane is chilly.
  • Headphones: Noise-canceling headphones will help you listen to music, watch films and get some quality rest without the distraction of other passengers.

  • Snacks: Pack some healthy snacks you like such as nuts, protein bars or fruit for when hunger strikes.
  • Entertainment: Don't forget to bring a book, magazine, tablet or portable games console to keep you busy and entertained during flight.

By packing these essentials, you'll be well on your way to experiencing a comfortable, enjoyable flight!




Tips for reducing stress during air travel:

  • Pack lightly. Heavy suitcases can add to your pre-travel stress. Pare down to only the necessities and you'll be glad you did.
  • Plan ahead. When you know the specifics of the flight, researching them ahead of time will help to reduce stress. Be sure to take into account flight times, ticket changes, and connecting flights.
  • Don't let your worries escalate. Anxiety levels can get higher the more you think about the flight. Instead, focus on the positive aspects of your journey, such as the destination or the fact that you get to travel.
  • Bring something to relax you. Whether it be a book, music, podcasts, or video games, bringing something that can help you pass the time (and take your mind off of your stress) can be really helpful.
  • Have snacks and drinks ready. By bringing along snacks and drinks, you'll be prepared in case of delays or turbulence. You'll have something to refuel and calm your nerves.
  • Dress comfortably. That bulky jacket will be a nuisance during a long flight. Look for clothing that won't constrict and that you can move around in. This can help you feel more relaxed and comfortable.
  • Ask for help. If you're feeling overwhelmed, don't hesitate to ask airport personnel or your fellow passengers for help. You don't need to tackle your worries alone.

Following these tips can help to reduce stress and make your air travel a more enjoyable experience. Bon voyage!
